
====================================================
CORE PROBLEM TO SOLVE
====================================================

Some users physically cover the camera with tape, fingers, cloth, paper, or objects,
yet the system still detects a face due to cached frames or low-quality detection.

This must be prevented.

====================================================
CAMERA INTEGRITY REQUIREMENTS
====================================================

The system must continuously verify:
- Camera is ON
- Camera lens is NOT covered
- Camera feed is LIVE (not frozen)
- Camera feed is CLEAR enough for identification
- Camera feed shows a real human face

These checks must work:
- Before exam verification
- During system requirement checks
- Continuously throughout the exam

====================================================
PRE-EXAM CAMERA VERIFICATION (MANDATORY)
====================================================

Before exam starts:
- Force live camera movement validation
- Ask user to perform random actions:
  - Blink
  - Turn head left/right
  - Move closer to camera
- Validate frame-to-frame motion
- Validate brightness and contrast levels
- Detect abnormal darkness or uniform color
- Detect lens obstruction patterns
- Reject static or fake camera feeds

If camera is covered or manipulated:
- Block exam start
- Show clear error message:
  “Camera is blocked or covered. Please clear the camera to continue.”

====================================================
REAL-TIME CAMERA MONITORING DURING EXAM
====================================================

Continuously (every few seconds):
- Check frame variance (to detect frozen feed)
- Check brightness histogram (to detect covered lens)
- Detect sudden darkness or color flattening
- Detect obstruction patterns (finger, tape, cloth)
- Verify live face movement over time
- Validate face depth cues (near/far changes)

====================================================
CAMERA COVER / HIDE DETECTION CONDITIONS
====================================================

Trigger CAMERA COVER VIOLATION if:
- Frame suddenly becomes dark or uniformly colored
- Face confidence drops abruptly
- Camera feed loses depth or motion
- Camera feed remains static beyond allowed threshold
- Sudden blur persists beyond tolerance
- Partial obstruction detected (finger, tape, shadow)

====================================================
VIOLATION HANDLING (REAL TIME)
====================================================

On detection:
- Instantly log a CAMERA COVER VIOLATION
- Capture and store a snapshot
- Timestamp the event
- Show live warning to candidate:
  “Camera obstruction detected. Please remove obstruction immediately.”

If obstruction persists:
- Increase violation severity
- Trigger auto actions:
  - Multiple warnings
  - Flag candidate
  - Force exam submission (if threshold crossed)

====================================================
DURING EXAM RULES
====================================================

- Exam MUST continue only if camera integrity is restored
- Violations must be visible in live violation feed
- Camera checks must NEVER pause during exam
- Disabling camera automatically counts as a violation
- Repeated camera blocking escalates severity

====================================================
TRANSPARENCY & FAIRNESS
====================================================

- Clearly indicate camera status to candidate
- Show camera integrity warnings non-intrusively
- Do not falsely accuse — use multiple checks before violation
- All events must be auditable after exam
